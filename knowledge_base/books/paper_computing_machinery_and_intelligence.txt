# Computing Machinery and Intelligence
# Author: Turing 1950
# Citation: Turing, A.M. (1950). Computing Machinery and Intelligence. Mind, 59(236), 433-460.

## Abstract
Turing proposes replacing 'Can machines think?' with the Imitation Game. He surveys objections (theological, mathematical, Lady Lovelace, consciousness, informality, ESP) and argues learning machines could surpass human intelligence.

## Key concepts
- Imitation Game (Turing Test)
- Learning machines vs programmed rules
- Child machine and education by reward/punishment
- Objections to machine intelligence systematically refuted

## Why it matters for ZION agents
The question 'Can machines think?' — foundational for agent self-inquiry
